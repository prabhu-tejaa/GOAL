## 1. Professional Background & "X-Factor" (5–10 mins)

Indeed recruiters look for **Product-Focused Engineers** who care about the "why" behind the code.

- **"Can you give me the rundown of your background?"**
    
- **"What is your primary programming language, and how much are you coding on a daily basis?"**
    
- **"Walk me through a project from your resume where you had to make a technical trade-off for a business impact."**
    
- **"I see you've worked in IoT at Siemens—how does that experience translate to a high-scale web platform like Indeed?"** (A likely customized question for your resume).
    

## 2. Mission & Value-Based Questions (10–15 mins)

Indeed relies heavily on these to filter for "cultural add".

- **"Our mission is to help people get jobs. What does that mission mean to you personally?"**
    
- **"Tell me about a time you showed compassion or patience toward a teammate."**
    
- **"How do you typically react to receiving negative feedback on a project?"**
    
- **"Describe a time traditional troubleshooting couldn't solve an issue. What innovative approach did you take?"**
    
- **"Tell me about a project you led. What were the success metrics, and how did it conclude?"**
    

## 3. The "AI-Assisted" & Remote Competency (5 mins)

This is a high priority in the **2026 Job Description**.

- **"How do you use LLM tools (like Copilot or ChatGPT) to accelerate your code delivery?"**
    
- **"Are you comfortable operating autonomously in a remote environment with minimal guidance?"**
    
- **"What AI-driven tools or models have you explored recently?"**
    

## 4. Logistical & "Red Flag" Checks (5 mins)

- **"Are you currently authorized to work in India, and do you require sponsorship?"**
    
- **"What is your expected salary range?"** (Note: The JD lists **2.16M - 3.24M INR** for this role).
    
- **"What is your current notice period?"** * **"Why Indeed specifically, rather than another big tech firm?"**
